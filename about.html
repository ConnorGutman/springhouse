Our Story

Springhouse creates value by recognizing real estate opportunities before others do, understanding what is possible, and then applying our expertise in investment, operations, and management to unlock their full potential and deliver exceptional returns.

We focus on investments that are undervalued or ineffectively managed - properties that may on the surface appear challenging, but with the right application of design and operational savvy, can be turned around efficiently and generate long-term value.

Our approach is comprehensive. We consider every factor, from access to emerging consumer markets to neighborhood trends to cultural factors.

We partner with the top architects, designers, and contractors. We use our asset and property management expertise to ensure each of our properties generate maximum premiums against costs and investment.

Adam Verner

Adam Verner is the Founder and CEO of Springhouse Partners. In this capacity, he leads corporate strategy and execution for the firmâ€™s principal investment transactions. Since its inception in 2010, Adam has successfully closed in excess of $400 million of real estate assets across the mixed-use, multifamily and hotel sectors.

Prior to forming Springhouse, Adam was the Director of Acquisitions at Urban American, an owner and operator of 12,000 apartments in the New York Metro area. In this role, he led the $75 million acquisition of the Westchester Plaza, a 1,000 unit mixed-use development.

Adam was also responsible for the management and oversight of the Grand Review portfolio, a 20 building collection of mixed-use assets spread throughout New York City.

Prior to joining Urban American, Adam was responsible for originating $500 million of commercial real estate equity and debt investments at Marathon Asset Management, a $10 billion hedge fund which specialized in opportunistic investments across all property types. Notable projects included the $3 billion Cosmopolitan Resort & Casino development in Las Vegas, the high-end condominium conversion of 147 Waverly Place in Manhattan and the initial zoning & land use phase for the Domino Sugar Factory redevelopment in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. Adam graduated with a business degree from American University in Washington, DC.
